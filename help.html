How to calculate GPA & CGPA in Nigeria

Simple Explanation

What is GPA?

GPA means Grade Point Average. It shows the average of your course scores for one semester only.

How it works in Nigeria

Each course has:

  • Units/Credit load (e.g. 2 units, 3 units)
  • Grade (A, B, C, etc.)
  • Grade points (based on the grade)

Typical Grade System

Grade Score Range Grade Point
A 70–100 5
B 60–69 4
C 50–59 3
D 45–49 2
E 40–44 1
F 0–39 0

How GPA is Calculated

  1. 1

    Multiply course units × grade point = Quality Points.

  2. 2

    Add all the Quality Points.

  3. 3

    Divide by the total course units for that semester.

Example GPA Calculation

Course Units Grade GP QP
Math 3 B 4 12
English 2 A 5 10
Physics 3 C 3 9
GNS 2 B 4 8

Total Quality Points = 39 | Total Units = 10

GPA = 3.9

What is CGPA?

CGPA means Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is your overall GPA from year 1 till your current semester.

Formula

CGPA = Total Quality Points (All) ÷ Total Units (All)

Example CGPA Calculation

1st Semester

QP: 39

Units: 10

2nd Semester

QP: 37

Units: 8

Course Units Grade GP QP
Chemistry 3 A 5 15
Biology 3 B 4 12
GNS II 2 A 5 10

Total Overall Calculation

Total QP = 39 + 37 = 76
Total Units = 10 + 8 = 18

CGPA = 4.22

Simple Summary

  • GPA = how well you did in one semester.

  • CGPA = your overall performance from year 1 till now.

  • Both are calculated using: Total Points ÷ Total Units.
